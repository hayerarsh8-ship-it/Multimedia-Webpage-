Phase 2: The Romantic Revolution Gallery

This gallery shows how art moved away from strict rules and logic. Instead, it focuses on strong personal feelings, imagination, and people fighting back against unfair control.

Wanderer above the Sea of Fog

Caspar David Friedrich — This painting shows a lonely person looking out at a huge, foggy view. It represents how deep and complicated our inner thoughts can feel when we look at huge spaces in nature.

Liberty Leading the People

Eugène Delacroix — This shows people fighting for their freedom during a revolution. The artist leaves behind calm styles to show the wild, loud energy of regular people coming together to stand up for themselves.

The Nightmare

Henry Fuseli — A look into our scary dreams and thoughts. By focusing on a bad dream, this painting shows the dark feelings and fears inside our minds that logic cannot easily explain.

The Slave Ship

J.M.W. Turner — A powerful painting that shows how cruel humans can be to each other, mixed with a wild, angry ocean. The bright colors and messy paint make the tragedy feel very real and scary.

The Ancient of Days

William Blake — This artwork shows a god-like figure creating the world using strict math tools. The artist wanted to criticize tight rules and show that the creative mind should be completely free instead.

The Third of May 1808

Francisco Goya — A sad and direct look at soldiers shooting innocent citizens. The artist does not make war look heroic; instead, he shows the scary, cold reality of violence against normal people.

Phase 4: Nature Log & Excursion

Field Observation: The Sublime in the Quiet Margins

This part records a simple walk outside into nature to take a close look at a single yellow flower growing in the grass. Instead of using science books or looking up strict definitions, this page focuses on how it feels to see nature directly. It shows that taking a moment to notice small details in the world can be incredibly peaceful and meaningful.
